# Pre-Build Research Task 2: Competitor & Landscape Analysis

**Project:** Dealer Ops Daily (working title) — daily AI-edited newsletter for U.S. franchised and independent auto-dealership operators (F&I directors, sales managers, desk managers, GMs, compliance/CIT leads).
**Operator:** DealerIntel (auto-dealer SaaS) — domain authority + warm seed list.
**Date:** 2026-04-20.
**Purpose:** map the existing dealer-newsletter landscape, find the gaps, and lock a positioning statement sharp enough to put on the about page.

---

## 1. The competitive set at a glance

The U.S. auto-dealer email-news market is structurally a barbell: one venture-scale modern brand (CDG) sitting on top of a handful of legacy trade publishers (Crain, Informa, Bobit, Cherokee, NADA, EVT) plus a long tail of vendor-owned blogs and LinkedIn newsletters. Almost nothing in the middle is built natively for the email-first, mobile-first, time-poor *operator* — the assistant GM, F&I director, or used-car desk manager who needs to know what changed yesterday in five minutes flat.

Sources used throughout: beehiiv blog roundups, the publishers' own media kits and subscribe pages, Growth In Reverse's profile of Yossi Levi, and the publishers' own LinkedIn pages.

## 2. Top players, dissected

### 2.1 Car Dealership Guy News (CDG News) — the 800-lb gorilla
- **Subscribers:** 55,000–65,000 daily readers (CDG's own subscribe page claims 55K+; Growth In Reverse profile from 2024 reports 65K newsletter readers and 455K+ X followers, now 550K+) ([CDG subscribe page](https://news.dealershipguy.com/subscribe), [Growth In Reverse — Yossi Levi](https://growthinreverse.com/yossi-levi/)).
- **Frequency / format:** Daily M–F. Curated bullets + one or two original reported pieces per week. Hooky subject lines, conversational voice, heavy use of dealer-quote pull blocks. Built on beehiiv.
- **Monetization:** Free to readers; revenue from in-newsletter sponsor placements, podcast sponsors, a $1,000/mo "featured" tier on the CDG Job Board (and $2,500/mo for >10 listings), and a recruiting arm launched Sep 2024 ([CDG: recruiting expansion](https://news.dealershipguy.com/p/car-dealership-guy-boldly-expands-into-professional-recruiting-2024-09-03)). Bootstrapped, no VC ([Growth In Reverse](https://growthinreverse.com/yossi-levi/)).
- **Open rate:** Not publicly disclosed; B2B niche newsletters at this scale typically run 35–45%.
- **Growth tactics:** Founder-led organic on X/LinkedIn (anonymous-to-deanonymized "personality" play), podcast cross-promotion (Spotify + Apple), live shows, the Job Board as a top-of-funnel hook, and PR via mainstream outlets.
- **Voice:** Insider, casual, "I called three dealers about this." Strong brand POV without being preachy.
- **Weakness for our purposes:** CDG is *industry-wide* — covers OEMs, lenders, tech vendors, M&A, EV policy, dealer principals. It is NOT optimized for the day-to-day desk/F&I/fixed-ops operator running deals this afternoon.

### 2.2 Automotive News (Crain) — the legacy paper of record
- **Subscribers (paid print):** 62,882 paid; 186,000 readers per issue; 309,000 newsletter opt-ins at 44% average open rate; 851K monthly uniques on site ([Automotive News media kit info](https://help.autonews.com/hc/en-us/articles/14999685553044-Media-Kit), [Crain — Automotive News](https://www.crain.com/brands/automotivenews/)).
- **Frequency / format:** Daily email (free opt-in tier) + paid weekly print + premium digital subscription (Shift, Mobility Report, Daily Drive, etc.) ([AN Daily newsletter](https://www.autonews.com/newsletters/daily/), [Shift signup](https://www.autonews.com/newsletter-signup-shift/)).
- **Monetization:** Paid subscription wall ($199–$249/yr typical), premium classified/recruitment ads (see [2025 Classified Media Kit](https://s3.amazonaws.com/crain-platform-autonews-assets/assets/pdf/mediakit/2025-AN-Classified-Media-Kit-v3.pdf)), event sponsorships (Retail Forum, World Congress).
- **Open rate:** 44% average (disclosed in media kit).
- **Growth tactics:** Brand inertia + event funnel + paid acquisition into newsletter free tier, then upsell to paid.
- **Voice:** Newsroom-formal, multi-source, OEM/manufacturer-heavy. Long stories.
- **Weakness:** Slow, paywalled, written for dealer principals and OEM executives — not the working operator. Paywall is a hard friction wall for store-level managers.

### 2.3 WardsAuto / Automotive Dive (Informa TechTarget) — merged trade
- **Subscribers:** Specific count not disclosed; consolidated as of Oct 2025 — Informa folded Automotive Dive subscribers into the new WardsAuto.com and unified newsletter list ([WardsAuto + Automotive Dive merger](https://www.wardsauto.com/news/wardsauto-and-automotive-dive-combine-new-site-and-newsletter-coming-soon/798977/)).
- **Frequency / format:** **WardsAuto Daily Edition** (industry-wide) and **WardsAuto Dealer Weekly** (Wednesdays, dealer-focused — combines former Dealer 5, Dealer Edition, Dealer Fixed Ops, and Dealer F&I lists) ([WardsAuto signup](https://www.wardsauto.com/signup/)). Free.
- **Monetization:** Display + sponsored content + lead-gen via Informa's B2B martech stack. Subsidized by parent.
- **Voice:** Trade-magazine analytical, OEM-supply-chain heavy, less deal-floor practical.
- **Weakness:** Weekly cadence on the dealer cut means it's not in the operator's morning routine. Dealer F&I content was already de-emphasized in the merger.

### 2.4 NADA Headlines — association daily
- **Subscribers:** Not publicly disclosed; NADA membership is ~16,000 franchised dealer rooftops, so the addressable pool is large but the email file is conservatively in the tens of thousands ([NADA Headlines](https://www.nada.org/nada/news/nada-headlines)).
- **Frequency / format:** Daily aggregator — pulls top auto-industry headlines from third-party outlets. Minimal original reporting.
- **Monetization:** Subsidized by NADA dues; promotes NADA Show, NADA U training, ATD content, and policy advocacy.
- **Voice:** Institutional, neutral, advocacy-tinged when policy is involved.
- **Weakness:** Aggregator-only — no original analysis, no operator angle, no tactics. It's a clipping service.

### 2.5 CBT News — the video-first dealer network
- **Subscribers:** "Thousands" claimed; LinkedIn page sits at the mid-tens-of-thousands range. Specific email file size not disclosed ([CBT News About](https://www.cbtnews.com/about-us/), [CBT News LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/company/cbt-news)).
- **Frequency / format:** Daily Newscast (video) + weekly email roundup + 13 ongoing shows covering sales, F&I, fixed ops, service drive ([CBT Daily Newscast](https://www.cbtnews.com/daily-newscast/)).
- **Monetization:** Vendor sponsorships, OEM-adjacent ad packages, NADA-show co-marketing.
- **Voice:** Broadcast-style, interview-heavy, vendor-friendly.
- **Weakness:** Video-first means the email is essentially a TV-guide for video assets, not a standalone read. Operators can't skim it during a coffee break.

### 2.6 F&I and Showroom (Bobit) + Auto Dealer Today
- **Subscribers:** Not publicly disclosed. Bobit Business Media bundles F&I and Showroom subs with Auto Dealer Today (free print + digital) ([F&I and Showroom subscribe](https://www.fi-magazine.com/subscribe), [Auto Dealer Today — BBM audience](https://www.bobit.com/audiences/autodealer-today/)).
- **Frequency / format:** Multiple opt-in eNewsletters by topic (F&I, sales, compliance) ([F&I newsletter signup](https://www.fi-magazine.com/newsletter-signup), [Auto Dealer Today newsletter signup](https://www.autodealertodaymagazine.com/pages/newsletter-signup)). Mix of weekly and event-driven.
- **Monetization:** Vendor display + sponsored product comparisons + compliance webinars.
- **Voice:** Trade-magazine, "best practices" oriented, compliance-heavy, leans long-form.
- **Weakness:** No daily tempo; the email is a digest of the website, not a standalone product. Heavy banner-ad load drags engagement.

### 2.7 Auto Remarketing / SubPrime Auto Finance News / BHPH Report (Cherokee Media Group)
- **Subscribers:** SubPrime Auto Finance News claims **47,000 weekday email subscribers** plus 30,000 print on a bi-monthly cycle ([SubPrime — Auto Remarketing Power 200 page](https://www.autoremarketing.com/power_200/subprime-auto-finance-news/)).
- **Frequency / format:** Daily email for Auto Remarketing and SubPrime; bi-monthly print magazines; podcast and Used Car Week event ([Auto Remarketing newsletter info](https://www.autoremarketing.com/ar/), [Cherokee Media Group](https://cherokeemediagroup.com/)).
- **Monetization:** Paid print sub ($24.95/yr historical), event revenue (Used Car Week is the cash cow), vendor display, OEM-captive sponsorship.
- **Voice:** Trade-formal, finance-and-remarketing focused. Strong on auction data, lender news, compliance updates from the CFPB/FTC angle.
- **Weakness:** Used-car/wholesale-skew; weak coverage of front-end variable-ops desking and new-car F&I product strategy. Print-era voice.

### 2.8 Cox Automotive / Manheim Insights
- **Subscribers:** Not publicly disclosed.
- **Frequency / format:** Weekly insights email + monthly Manheim Used Vehicle Value Index release + quarterly call invitations ([Manheim Insights](https://www.coxautoinc.com/insights/manheim-insights/)).
- **Monetization:** None directly — owned-media for Cox Automotive products (Manheim, vAuto, Kelley Blue Book, Dealertrack, Xtime, Autotrader). Pure top-of-funnel.
- **Voice:** Economist/data-analyst, neutral, charts-first.
- **Weakness:** It's a vendor's own-media. Operators read it for the MUVVI number and bounce. No editorial voice, no operator-tactical content.

### 2.9 Dealer Marketing Magazine + Digital Dealer Magazine
- **Subscribers:** Dealer Marketing Magazine — ~3,663 LinkedIn followers ([Dealer Marketing Magazine LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/company/dealer-marketing-magazine)). Digital Dealer claims ~19,500 magazine readers ([Digital Dealer subscribers](https://digitaldealer.com/dealer-magazine-subscribers/), [Digital Dealer magazine](https://digitaldealer.com/magazine/)).
- **Frequency / format:** Weekly e-newsletter + bi-monthly print + flagship Digital Dealer Conference (Tampa/Vegas).
- **Monetization:** Event-driven (DDC sponsorships), vendor advertising.
- **Voice:** Vendor-friendly thought leadership, light reporting.
- **Weakness:** Marketing-tech and digital-retail focused; thin on F&I, fixed ops, desking.

### 2.10 Beehiiv-published auto newsletters (the long tail)
- The beehiiv blog's "Top 10 Automotive Newsletters to Sponsor" roundup ([beehiiv blog](https://www.beehiiv.com/blog/top-10-automotive-newsletters-to-sponsor-2024)) lists EV/transport/luxury titles — Future of Transportation (10K+ auto execs, Reilly Brennan), The EV Universe (7K+), Highway Hustle (75K+, but trucking), The Rivian Report, Electric Avenue, The Car Investor, Mobility EVo, Four Wheel Trends. **None target U.S. franchised/independent auto-dealer operators.** AutoVerse and The Danis Auto Newsletter exist on beehiiv but are consumer-leaning ([AutoVerse](https://theautoversenewsletter.beehiiv.com/), [Danis Auto](https://danisauto.beehiiv.com/)).
- Substack auto-dealer pubs are similarly thin — The Automotive Advantage and The Automotive Advisor are visible but small ([search results page](https://www.letterpal.io/blog/top-15-automotive-newsletters)).
- LinkedIn newsletters in this space are mostly vendor-owned thought-leadership (CDK, Reynolds, ComplyAuto, Dealertrack) and individual consultants. No category-defining title in F&I, desking, or fixed-ops on LinkedIn at scale.

## 4. Gap analysis — where the field is weak

**Gap 1 — Daily, operator-tactical, store-floor voice.** CDG owns the daily-tempo "modern" slot but plays at the industry/policy/M&A altitude. Everything else daily (Automotive News, NADA Headlines, SubPrime) is either paywalled, executive-pitched, or aggregator-thin. Nobody is writing for the assistant GM running today's deals.

**Gap 2 — F&I as a daily beat, not a quarterly feature.** F&I and Showroom is the deepest F&I title, but it ships multi-weekly, runs long-form, and has the trade-magazine pacing problem. There is no "F&I morning briefing" with yesterday's CFPB/FTC moves, product-penetration trends, lender rate sheets, and chargeback intel in a 5-minute read.

**Gap 3 — Compliance as an editorial center, not a webinar add-on.** Post-2024 FTC CARS Rule activity, the March 2026 FTC warning letters to 97 dealer groups (covering 500+ rooftops) ([CBT — FTC compliance pressure](https://www.cbtnews.com/ftc-compliance-pressure-pricing-discrepancies/)), state-level fee disclosure laws, and the rise of vendors like ComplyAuto have made compliance a daily operational concern. No newsletter treats it as a daily beat.

**Gap 4 — Used-car *desking* (not just wholesale).** Cherokee owns wholesale/remarketing. CDG covers used-car macro. Nobody owns the daily "what should I be paying / asking / floor-planning today" question for the used-car desk manager — the role that determines store profitability.

**Gap 5 — Variable ops vs. fixed ops separation.** Most pubs lump everything together. WardsAuto specifically *consolidated* its Dealer Fixed Ops and Dealer F&I newsletters into one Wednesday email — a tell that the legacy publishers are de-resourcing this audience, not investing in it.

**Gap 6 — AI-edited speed and consistency.** The legacy publishers have human bureaus and slow tempo. CDG has a small founder-led team. None has publicly built an AI-edited pipeline that can ship a tight, factual, voice-consistent issue 5×/week with sub-30-min lead time on breaking news. This is exactly the gap a DealerIntel-operated, AI-assisted product can attack.

**Gap 7 — The "no parent company" trust angle.** Cox/Manheim is owned-media. Cherokee runs its own auctions and events. Bobit, Crain, Informa each carry vendor-relationship baggage. CDG is the only big independent — and it's already large enough that vendor relationships now shape coverage. There's room for a *second* independent voice, particularly one anchored in a SaaS operator with technical/data credibility.

**Underserved sub-segments to attack first:**
1. **F&I directors at single-rooftop and small-group stores** — too small for AN paid sub, ignored by NADA.
2. **Used-car desk managers** — tactical daily price/turn intel, not wholesale-index recap.
3. **Compliance/CIT leads** — the FTC, CFPB, and state-AG regulatory tempo deserves a daily beat.
4. **Sales managers in 5–25 store groups** — the "middle market" that isn't a public 100-store group but needs better intel than NADA Headlines.

## 5. Positioning statement (about-page draft)

> **Dealer Ops Daily is the five-minute morning briefing for the people actually running U.S. car dealerships — F&I directors, desk managers, and floor GMs. We cover what changed yesterday in compliance, F&I products, used-car values, and store ops, with the speed of a modern email brand and the operator credibility of a working dealer-tech company. No paywall, no parent-OEM agenda, no thirty-paragraph trade-mag features — just the signal you need before your first save call.**

This deliberately distinguishes us from CDG (which plays at industry/macro altitude), from Automotive News (paywalled, executive-pitched, slow), from NADA Headlines (aggregator with no analysis), and from the Bobit/Cherokee trade titles (vendor-influenced, weekly tempo, long-form). The "operator credibility of a working dealer-tech company" line is the DealerIntel moat — no other newsletter in this market has a SaaS company's data and customer base behind it.
