# Niche Selection Analysis — Pre-Build Research Task 1

**Date:** 2026-04-20
**Purpose:** Score 8 candidate niches across 5 criteria to recommend ONE niche for a daily, AI-written, hyper-niche beehiiv newsletter targeting 10K subs / 30%+ open rate within 12 months.
**Operator profile:** Runs DealerIntel (auto dealer software) and a Claude Code workflow practice. Strong authority in automotive verticals (#1, #3, #7) and AI dev tooling (#6). Moderate authority in adjacent sales/SaaS topics (#2, #5, #8).

---

## Scoring Criteria (1-10 each, /50 total)

- **(a) CPM / ad rates** — Typical 2026 sponsor CPMs in the vertical
- **(b) Competition** — Density and quality of incumbents (10 = open field, 1 = saturated)
- **(c) Subscriber acquisition cost** — Ease/cost of growth via Boosts, SparkLoop, organic referral
- **(d) Operator domain authority** — Existing credibility/asset leverage
- **(e) Longevity** — Will the topic still matter in 3+ years (10 = durable, 1 = fad)

## Per-Niche Rationale

### 1. Dealer Ops Daily — 41/50

**(a) CPM 9/10:** Auto dealer audience is one of the most monetizable B2B segments. Dealer-targeted publishers like Car Dealership Guy News (CDG, ~55K subs, 180K-pro network) command premium auto-vendor sponsorship rates. F&I, DMS, CRM, lender, warranty, and reinsurance vendors all spend aggressively. Niche B2B newsletters in this caliber regularly sell at $75-$200 effective CPM, with single-placement deals at 8K subs typically clearing $1,500-$2,500.

**(b) Competition 6/10:** CDG dominates the daily dealer brief category. Auto Remarketing, Automotive News Daily 5, F&I and Showroom, CBT News, and Digital Dealer all compete. However, none focus specifically on the F&I + sales manager + desking + compliance intersection — that's a tighter operational wedge that incumbents only partially cover.

**(c) SAC 7/10:** Auto dealer operators are reachable via boosts from CDG-adjacent newsletters, NADA-related lists, and trade publications. SparkLoop CPAs in B2B verticals run $1.50-$5 per qualified sub. Operator's DealerIntel customer base provides a warm seed list and built-in credibility for cross-promotion.

**(d) Authority 10/10:** Maximum leverage. DealerIntel is in-vertical software; the operator already has dealer relationships, vendor contacts, and product credibility. Newsletter doubles as top-of-funnel for the SaaS.

**(e) Longevity 9/10:** Auto retail has been a stable B2B category for 50+ years. EV transition, F&I product evolution, regulatory shifts (CFPB, FTC Vehicle Shopping Rule) ensure 3+ years of news flow. Risk: dealer consolidation slowly shrinks TAM, but absolute market remains huge.

---

### 2. Field Sales Intel — 35/50

**(a) CPM 8/10:** D2D verticals (solar $30B, roofing $56B, pest $12.6B, HVAC) have aggressive sponsor budgets — CRM tools (SalesRabbit, Knockio), training programs (D2D Experts, D2DU), and SaaS (Leadbeam, Fieldpie) all want this audience. Reps earn $80K-$180K, justifying premium CPMs.

**(b) Competition 9/10:** Surprisingly thin newsletter coverage. D2DCon and D2D Experts are events/training-led. No dominant daily roundup exists across all 4 sub-verticals — this is a genuinely open lane.

**(c) SAC 6/10:** Reaching reps individually is harder than reaching dealer-principals. Most growth would come from D2D Facebook groups, podcast cross-promo, and event sponsorships rather than newsletter-network boosts. SparkLoop ecosystem in this vertical is thin.

**(d) Authority 5/10:** Operator has no D2D track record. Would need to build credibility from zero — possible but slower.

**(e) Longevity 7/10:** D2D has cycled before; 2026 data shows it's still profitable but threatened by digital alternatives and regulatory backlash (esp. solar). 3-year horizon: yes, 5+ year: uncertain.

---

### 3. RV Dealer Brief — 34/50

**(a) CPM 7/10:** RVDA member base is small (~2,500 dealers nationally). Sponsor pool is real (RV manufacturers, F&I providers, lenders, lot-management software) but smaller than auto. Effective CPMs respectable but ceiling is lower.

**(b) Competition 8/10:** RV Executive Today (RVDA), RVBusiness, RV PRO, RV News, RV Dealer News — mostly print/web magazines. No dominant daily dealer brief. Open lane, but the lane is narrower.

**(c) SAC 4/10:** Tiny TAM. The entire universe of US RV dealers is roughly 2,500-3,000 rooftops. Hitting 10K subs requires expanding into adjacent audiences (manufacturer reps, suppliers, finance partners) which dilutes the niche.

**(d) Authority 9/10:** Operator's auto-dealer software experience translates strongly. F&I, DMS, lot ops are similar problem shapes.

**(e) Longevity 6/10:** RV market is cyclical and rate-sensitive. Post-COVID hangover still working through inventory. Industry will exist in 3 years but volatility hurts predictability.

---

### 4. Indie App Revenue — 27/50

**(a) CPM 6/10:** Indie app operator audience is small and notoriously cheap. Paying tools: ASO platforms, RevenueCat, Superwall, Adapty. Limited deep-pocketed sponsors.

**(b) Competition 5/10:** Indie Hackers, This Week in Apps (Sarah Perez), Mobile Dev Memo, RevenueCat blog, Heavybit content. Crowded.

**(c) SAC 6/10:** Indie Hackers/X organic potential is decent. SparkLoop dev-newsletter ecosystem is active.

**(d) Authority 3/10:** No claimed background here. Would be building from scratch.

**(e) Longevity 7/10:** App economy persists but indie portfolio model faces platform risk (Apple policy changes, Vision Pro pivot, AI-generated app glut).

---

### 5. The Vertical SaaS Report — 34/50

**(a) CPM 9/10:** B2B SaaS audience is the highest-CPM category alongside finance. VC firms, dev tools, enterprise sales platforms all sponsor. Sub for sub, vertical SaaS execs are the most valuable readers in the candidate set.

**(b) Competition 4/10:** Tom Tunguz (150K+ subs), SaaStr, Euclid Ventures, Sacra, The Generalist, Lenny's Newsletter (1M+) all overlap. Highly contested category. A dedicated "vertical SaaS only" angle is possible but executive readers already consume horizontal coverage.

**(c) SAC 6/10:** SparkLoop business/SaaS recommendation network is mature. Boost CPAs around $2-4. Many adjacent newsletters exist for cross-promo.

**(d) Authority 6/10:** Vertical SaaS is adjacent — DealerIntel IS vertical SaaS — but operator has no public M&A/funding analysis brand.

**(e) Longevity 9/10:** Vertical SaaS market valued ~$95B in 2026 and growing 250%+ faster than horizontal SaaS. Decade-long thesis.

### 6. Claude Code Weekly — 33/50

**(a) CPM 7/10:** Developer audience monetizes well via dev tools (databases, observability, AI APIs). However, AI dev tooling sponsors are still figuring out attribution. Free-tier developer audience hurts conversion math.

**(b) Competition 4/10:** ClaudeLog, AI Maker Substack, Code with Andrea, Pragmatic Engineer (general), TLDR AI all overlap. The space is exploding — by EOY 2026 expect 20+ Claude/agent-specific newsletters. Saturation risk is real.

**(c) SAC 8/10:** Developer newsletter ecosystem (TLDR, Bytes, JavaScript Weekly, Pragmatic Engineer) is the most mature SparkLoop/Boost network. Cheap acquisition.

**(d) Authority 9/10:** Operator runs a Claude Code practice — strong authority and shippable proof-of-work content (workflows, hooks, skills).

**(e) Longevity 5/10:** "Claude Code" as a brand may not exist in 3 years. Anthropic could rename, fork, replace, or be displaced. Topic risk is high. The underlying "AI agents for developers" theme survives, but the niche framing is fragile.

### 7. AutoTech Pulse — 40/50

**(a) CPM 9/10:** Same vendor pool as #1 but skewed toward software buyers (GMs, dealer principals, IT, BDC managers). DMS/CRM/CDP/AI vendors have the deepest pockets in the auto stack — Tekion, CDK, Reynolds, Dealertrack, VinSolutions all sponsor heavily.

**(b) Competition 7/10:** No dedicated daily AutoTech newsletter. CDG covers it as part of broader brief; Auto News covers it as a beat. Open lane for a focused tech-only daily.

**(c) SAC 6/10:** Same channels as Dealer Ops Daily but slightly narrower target persona (tech-buying decision makers vs. all dealer ops).

**(d) Authority 10/10:** DealerIntel IS AutoTech — perfect product-market-author fit.

**(e) Longevity 8/10:** AI-in-dealership is the dominant 2026 theme (20+ NADA sessions on AI). Topic compounds for 3-5 years. Slight risk: tech consolidation could shrink vendor sponsor pool.

---

### 8. The Commission Report — 31/50

**(a) CPM 7/10:** High-income sales operators are a great audience but spread across industries (SaaS AEs, real estate, mortgage, D2D, auto, medical device). Sponsor pool: sales tools (Apollo, Outreach, Gong), training programs, productivity tools.

**(b) Competition 3/10:** Crowded. Sam Parr/The Hustle alums, Justin Welsh (LinkedIn-focused), Sales Hacker, Modern Sales, 30MPC, Pavilion, GTMnow, Brian LaManna. Hard to differentiate.

**(c) SAC 7/10:** Sales/business newsletter recommendation networks are the most active on SparkLoop alongside finance.

**(d) Authority 6/10:** Adjacent — operator sells to commission-driven dealer staff but isn't a known name in the broader sales-influencer scene.

**(e) Longevity 8/10:** "Quota-carrying salespeople" persist as long as commerce exists. Topic risk: AI may eliminate many SDR/AE roles by 2029.

## Recommendation

**RECOMMENDED NICHE: Dealer Ops Daily**

**Rationale:** Dealer Ops Daily wins on the most important compounding criteria: maximum operator authority (10/10), premium CPMs (9/10), and durable longevity (9/10). The operator's existing DealerIntel asset provides both a warm seed-subscriber list and embedded vendor relationships — the two hardest things to manufacture in a cold launch. CDG proves the audience pays attention to a daily dealer brief (~55K subs, 180K-pro network), and the operator can carve a tighter wedge inside that audience by going deeper on F&I + sales-manager + desking + compliance — operational topics CDG covers only at surface level.

The 12-month 10K/30%+ goal is realistic because:
1. **Seed list:** DealerIntel customers and prospects = a few hundred to a few thousand qualified opens on day one.
2. **CPM economics:** Even at 5K subs, vertical depth allows $1,500+ placements ($25-40 CPM effective at the small end, scaling to $75-150 by 10K).
3. **Content moat:** Operator has insider access (vendor releases, dealer feedback, software roadmaps) that pure-content competitors lack.
4. **Funnel synergy:** Newsletter doubles as top-of-funnel for DealerIntel SaaS, making subscriber LTV 2-5x higher than ad-only economics imply.

Primary risk: CDG is a strong incumbent. Mitigation = narrower vertical wedge (operations/F&I focus, not market commentary) and faster cadence on tactical content vs. CDG's broader market-commentary lane.

## Backup Choices (Ranked)

### Backup 1: AutoTech Pulse (40/50)
Nearly identical authority and CPM profile to Dealer Ops Daily but slightly narrower TAM and weaker daily-news flow. Best alternative if Dealer Ops Daily proves too crowded against CDG. Could also be launched as a sister/spinoff product after primary brand reaches scale. Risk: harder to fill 250 daily editions on a tech-only beat without padding.

### Backup 2: Field Sales Intel (35/50)
Highest "open field" score in the set — no dominant daily competitor across solar/roofing/pest/HVAC. Best play if operator wants pure greenfield with maximum sponsor whitespace. Drawbacks: zero existing authority means a 6-12 month credibility ramp, and audience is harder to reach via newsletter-network boosts.

### Backup 3: The Vertical SaaS Report (34/50)
Highest theoretical CPM ceiling and longest topic longevity. Loses on competition (Tom Tunguz, SaaStr, Lenny, Generalist already own this audience) and only moderate operator authority. Worth considering if the operator wants to angle toward acquisition-by-VC or fund-launch optionality, where this category over-indexes. Picked over RV Dealer Brief (34/50 tied) because RV's TAM ceiling caps growth below the 10K target, while Vertical SaaS has effectively unlimited TAM.
