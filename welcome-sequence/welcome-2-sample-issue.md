## ⚖️ Compliance

**FTC's CARS Rule enforcement: what your e-contracting workflow needs by Friday**

The FTC's Combatting Auto Retail Scams Rule remains in active enforcement, with three settlement actions in the last 90 days targeting add-on disclosure timing and "junk fee" inflation. The pattern across all three: stores that bundled GAP and VSC into a single line item without disaggregated price disclosure took the largest fines.

**What to do today:** Pull a sample of last week's deal jackets and check that every add-on has its own line, its own price, and its own customer-acknowledged disclosure timestamp. If your DMS rolls them into a "menu total," that's the exposure.

## 💰 F&I

**Q1 captive subprime approval rates: GMF and Ford Credit diverge**

GM Financial expanded subprime tier 4–5 approvals 9% YoY in Q1, while Ford Credit tightened the same band by 6%. Net effect for dealers running both: GMF deal flow up, Ford Credit pushing more deals to secondary lenders (Westlake, Ally).

**What to do today:** If your store runs both brands, re-rank your lender stack on the desking screen. Stores that left Ford Credit at the top tier of the waterfall through Q1 are sending 12–15% more deals back to F&I for re-shop than peers who re-ranked.

## 🚗 Used-car desking

**Manheim Used Vehicle Value Index: -1.4% MoM, sharper drop in compact SUV**

The April Manheim index posted a 1.4% month-over-month decline, with compact SUVs (-3.1%) leading the drop and full-size pickups (+0.6%) bucking the trend. Auction-to-retail spreads widened to ~$2,400 average, the widest since November.

**What to do today:** If you're sitting on >45 days' supply in compact SUV, your reprice cadence needs to move from weekly to twice-weekly. Stores already on dynamic pricing are absorbing the drop; stores still on weekly reprice are giving up an average $180/unit on the back end.

## 🏬 Store ops

**The 3-minute test for whether your BDC is actually qualifying leads**

Pull yesterday's BDC log. Count how many appointments were set with: (1) a budget range, (2) a trade-in disclosed, (3) a credit comfort level. Stores hitting all three on >60% of appointments are seeing 22% higher close rates from set-to-show; stores under 30% are essentially running an appointment factory with no qualification.

**What to do today:** If you're under 30%, the fix is usually a single coaching change — give the BDC a 4-question script and require it before the calendar invite goes out.
